KazUTB Completes Digitization of Rare 19th Century Manuscripts

A multi-year collaborative effort culminates in open access to over 5,000 pages of previously restricted historical texts, advancing digital humanities research.

The KazUTB Smart Library is proud to announce the successful completion of the "Echoes of Antiquity" digitization project. This ambitious initiative, spanning over three years, has resulted in the high-resolution scanning and metadata tagging of over 5,000 pages of rare 19th-century manuscripts, previously housed in restricted physical archives due to their fragile condition.

"Our goal was never just preservation; it was democratization of access," stated Dr. Alikhan Bokeikhanov, Director of Special Collections. "By bringing these texts into the digital realm with such high fidelity, we are allowing scholars worldwide to study the ink strokes, marginalia, and paper textures without risking further degradation to the physical items."

Project Highlights

  • Over 5,000 high-resolution TIFF images created.
  • Implementation of OCR (Optical Character Recognition) tailored for historical Cyrillic and Arabic scripts.
  • Creation of detailed metadata adhering to Dublin Core standards.
  • Integration with the KazUTB digital discovery layer for seamless searching.

The collection includes significant works on early Central Asian botany, regional diplomatic correspondence, and personal diaries of key historical figures. Researchers can now filter these documents by geographical origin, author, and specific historical events through the newly updated Discovery portal.

This project was made possible through a generous grant from the National Heritage Foundation and collaborative efforts with the Department of History and Digital Humanities. The KazUTB Smart Library remains committed to leveraging technology to protect and share academic treasures.

An exploration of modern methodologies for securing born-digital artifacts against technological obsolescence.

About the Event

The transition from physical to digital archives has presented curators with unprecedented challenges. While a manuscript from the 15th century might survive in a controlled environment, a digital file from the late 1990s is often unreadable due to hardware and software obsolescence.

This lecture will delve into the active interventions required to maintain the integrity and accessibility of digital artifacts. We will explore format migration strategies, emulation environments, and the ethical considerations of preserving born-digital content, from early web forums to contemporary social media discourses.

Attendees will gain insights into the KazUTB Smart Library's ongoing initiatives to establish a robust digital vault, ensuring that the ephemeral nature of today's digital creation does not lead to a "digital dark age" for future historians.

Abstract

This comprehensive text provides an advanced introduction to macroeconomic theory and policy. The fourth edition has been thoroughly revised to incorporate the latest theoretical developments and empirical evidence following recent global economic shifts. It uniquely blends rigorous mathematical modeling with deep historical context, making it essential for both undergraduate honors students and postgraduates in economics.

Key topics include endogenous growth theory, modern monetary policy frameworks under uncertainty, and the integration of financial frictions into dynamic stochastic general equilibrium (DSGE) models. The text consistently bridges the gap between abstract academic theory and practical central banking policy decisions.

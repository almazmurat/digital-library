# Methodology Review

Date: 2026-05-13
Review type: Internal mock methodology critique

## 1. Methodology Strengths

1. Clear phase-by-phase empirical pipeline (phase1 to phase3 with Intermediate Empirical Review transition).
2. Strong traceability culture linking requirements, artifacts, and evidence.
3. Explicit disclosure of bounded synthetic/local context limitations.
4. Balanced use of governance metrics and experiment metrics.
5. Reproducibility orientation via structured artifacts (CSV/JSON/docs/maps).

## 2. Methodology Weaknesses

1. External methodological anchoring is under-cited and placeholder-heavy.
2. Threat-to-validity treatment is not yet framed against a formal methodological template.
3. Some rationale statements remain implicit rather than explicitly criterion-driven.
4. The distinction between "program-defense methodology adequacy" and "publication-style methodological adequacy" is not yet made explicit in the paper body.

## 3. Reproducibility Risks

1. Environment sensitivity for timing metrics (local runtime context).
2. Bounded execution patterns may be difficult to compare with external studies.
3. Incomplete repeated-run histories for selected stability dimensions.

## 4. Missing Caveats to Add Explicitly

1. Clarify that figure-based synthesis indicators are comparative aids, not universal quality scales.
2. Clarify that mutation score utility depends on mutation design scope and test target boundaries.
3. Clarify that chaos scenarios validate bounded recovery behavior, not full operational resilience.

## 5. Defense Robustness Assessment

1. Yes, because methodology is transparent, traceable, and caveated.

### Strong enough for publication-style rigor

1. Not yet; requires citation completion, formalized validity framework, and tighter novelty-method linkage.

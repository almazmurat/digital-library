# Academic Quality Checklist

Date: 2026-05-13

## Research Framing

- [ ] Research problem is explicitly defined.
- [ ] Research objective and contribution are clearly stated.
- [ ] Literature review includes relevant and current references.

## Methodological Rigor

- [ ] Method section explains phase-by-phase design clearly.
- [ ] Data sources and collection constraints are transparent.
- [ ] Threats to validity are explicitly discussed.
- [ ] Reproducibility pathways are documented.

## Evidence Integrity

- [ ] All claims are traceable to factual repository artifacts.
- [ ] No fabricated metrics, references, or screenshots are present.
- [ ] Strength-of-support and caveats are included for major claims.

## Analytical Quality

- [ ] Results are descriptive before interpretive.
- [ ] Discussion explains why outcomes occurred, not only what occurred.
- [ ] Alternative explanations are considered where evidence is ambiguous.

## Presentation Quality

- [ ] Section transitions are coherent.
- [ ] Figure/table numbering and references are consistent.
- [ ] Citation style is consistent and complete.
- [ ] Language remains precise, formal, and concise.

## Part 3 Addendum (Review Rigor)

- [ ] Contribution novelty is explicitly differentiated from standard QA retrospective reporting.
- [ ] Threats-to-validity discussion follows a formal empirical framing approach.
- [ ] Figure captions include claim-level takeaway statements and scope caveats.
- [ ] program-defense readiness and publication-style readiness are explicitly distinguished.
- [ ] Citation placeholders are fully replaced in all literature-dependent sections before final submission.

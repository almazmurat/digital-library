# Preliminary Conclusions Draft

## 1. Consolidated Takeaways

1. The repository demonstrates a measurable QA evolution from risk baseline definition to experiment-backed synthesis.
2. Automation governance in phase2 improved control visibility but did not fully resolve critical risk concentrations.
3. Midterm reassessment provided necessary empirical correction and prevented static interpretation of early assumptions.
4. Phase3 experimental layers contributed additional reliability and resilience evidence beyond nominal-path automation outcomes.

## 2. Bounded Conclusion Statements

1. The evidence supports process-maturity growth across phases.
2. The evidence also supports persistence of integration-boundary risk as a key unresolved concern.
3. The evidence does not support claims of production-scale performance/resilience certainty due bounded execution contexts.

## 3. Immediate Research Implication

The strongest immediate implication is that multi-layer QA evidence produces better decision quality than relying only on automation pass rates or coverage presence metrics.
